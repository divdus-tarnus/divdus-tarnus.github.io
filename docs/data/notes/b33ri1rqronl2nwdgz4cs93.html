Managerialism

Managerialism is James Burnham's term to refer to the form of government adopted in the early 1900s by the Soviet Union, Nazi Germany, and the USA (to a lesser extent), replacing both capitalism and Lenin's attempt at socialism. To Burnham, each country had created a bureaucratic, technocratic class that was increasingly taking over the economy and displacing the previous ruling elite.

This formulation, to Burnham, explains the tendencies of each nation better than their nominal form.
